He made it for NASA as a way to complain for their poor outreach strategy, specially in social media. I even made a Twitter mention to Alan Ladwig (Asso. Administrator for Public Outreach at NASA HQ). I met him at this conference. Complaining is easy. But, what would then Reid Gower do? He doesn´t say. What would I do, then? I have quite some ideas, but I think is fair to share at least one. Here it is: Let kids and enthusiast announce and explain some of your discoveries. The main point in outreach is to bridge the gap between the 'Science world' and the 'Real world'. Surely a discovery will be extremely exciting, beautiful, and important for a researcher, but it won´t reach far if you can´t translate it. I am talking mainly about the language, but I am also talking about the viewpoint. The beauty the scientist sees involves much training, numbers and equation with meaning to him. You need to translate that, keeping the same passion.
